Olevirith's Guide to Manager
Manager is similar to the Overlord, but be more conservative when attacking, don't rush the opponent. Play more like a Necromancer, claw to maximize drones instead of directly attacking opponents.

1. Builds
I use the build of 0/0/2/7/7/6/4/7 for a few reasons. Regen isn't too important if you can hide well, and dodge. Max health is just dodging and dodging accuracy, so i dont inlude any, so I know exactly where I'm dodging to. The 2 body damage is to more tank a hit, than add to health, so i have durability. Durability combined with precision dodging is what I like to use. Then I use 7 bullet speed and 7 bullet penetration because of (drones). But with this particular manager build changes at the bullet damage. I use 6 bullet damage because it eliminates drones a small bit faster on contact with other things. The 7 pen is to last, and 6 damage doesnt make too much of a difference off of 7 because of Manager's coding buffs. So I attack with similar damage, and use the faster elimination as an advantage with the 4 reload. So i can manipulate the drones in the arena as a mix, and use it in many unique strats, because of all the possible utilizations of fast, consistent reload, as well as 7 movement speed. (which is almost standard on drone classes.) The 2 body damage is extremely useful for tanking a few hits, but without sacrificing much for that power.

2. Strategies
My strategy utilizes the 6 bullet damage and the 4 reload to make a consistent stream of new refreshing drones. Basically, I utilize the reload to create a drone swarm, (which is spread out) to confuse and misdirect the target while hiding myself. When the enemy makes a mistake, I take the fastest opportunity to attack and kill. Now, let me explain how I execute it, and how it is extremely effective.

The start is to spread out the drones, but this, however simple it seems, is the hardest and most crucial step. I place my mouse cursor behind the drones, but repelling them a bit wider than usual. I then turn them (to put it simply) and send them out in an arrow formation towards a possible location of the target. (I'm going to refer as the target as an overlord)

Next step I can do 2 things, but lets walk through each one.

I can:

1:
Follow the drones and charge into a [most likely] ready target. The reason I dont choose this choice often is because it is putting your own tank closer to the enemy drones/tank with your own drones in an unprepared repel position built to prepare for a standard claw. This is risky and possible in only a few situations, which are:

a: The target is unprepared.

b: I use the formation to form a pierce.

2:
Dodge to the side of my repel, and approach slower, while sending my drones further out into a repel to cover a large[r] area. I then stop and hide to wait for the target. When I spot the target, I let a drone of mine die, and that starts the fight (because of my reload.) I immediately spring into action and start to flee into a dodge; Then I attempt a claw. (sometimes works, sometimes doesnt.) I retreat and twist my drones into a kind of "Whirlpool" which keeps the drones in a constant motion. I try to use the reload for a constant stream to hide my location and when I spot the target, (in a prediction) I kill, and retract the full circle into a standard defense.

For defense, while most drones are away, its wise to keep a few of the newer regenerated drones close to your tank to protect against surprise attacks. We can do this by controlling our repel, and I can get into that later. Controlling the repel is a foundational technique to learn and best if it is utilized for the methods I make for drone classes. Back to Manager; the 4 reload is your main offense and only defense, if you cant destroy your own drones in a moment of danger, you die, so find your opponent's drones/bullets and pierce your own into them, that will regenerate your own drones while you retreat for a defense. 4 reload is perfectly timed for these kind of situations.
